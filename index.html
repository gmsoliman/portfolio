About Me

When I went to college, I wanted to be a doctor. After I quit pre-med, I knew I was destined to be a diplomat. Then I thought I'd be a cartoonist, so I became a zip line instructor. But, hey, I'm funny; I should be a stand-up comic. By the time I graduated, I was working at a nonprofit, dreaming of being an intelligence analyst. So, I got a job at a beer garden. Then a job monitoring mall security cameras, but I secretly knew I would quit and become a reporter. Now, I write code.

I strove to become a software developer to become all the different versions of myself that I once thought I was meant to be. Through code, I can be creative or expressive, solve problems, be analytical, methodical, have a sense of humor, and (fortunately) never reach a point where there's nothing left to learn or challenge me.
